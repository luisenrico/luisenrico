How to Make Paksiw na Isda: A Step-by-Step Guide

Paksiw na Isda is a simple yet delicious Filipino fish dish that’s perfect for lunch or dinner. Here’s how you can make Paksiw na Isda at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 medium-sized fish (such as mackerel, bangus/milkfish, tilapia, or any firm-fleshed fish), cleaned and sliced into serving pieces
  • 1 cup vinegar (cane vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2-3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis), or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 1-2 pieces long green chili peppers (siling pansigang) or siling haba (optional, for spiciness)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cooking oil

Step 1: Prepare the Fish

  • Clean and Slice: Rinse the fish under cold water and pat dry with paper towels. Slice the fish into serving pieces and set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

  • Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot or pan, heat a small amount of cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic and sliced ginger until fragrant.
  • Add Onions: Add sliced onions to the pot. Sauté until onions are translucent.

Step 3: Add Vinegar and Water

  • Pour Vinegar and Water: Pour vinegar and water into the pot. Add dried bay leaves, whole peppercorns, soy sauce, and fish sauce. Stir well to combine.

Step 4: Simmer the Sauce

  • Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 5: Add Fish and Chili Peppers

  • Add Fish: Carefully add the sliced fish pieces to the pot, making sure they are submerged in the vinegar mixture.
  • Add Chili Peppers: If using long green chili peppers or siling haba for spiciness, add them to the pot.

Step 6: Cook the Fish

  • Cook: Cover the pot and let the fish simmer gently over low heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is fully cooked and tender. Avoid stirring too much to prevent breaking the fish pieces.

Step 7: Adjust Seasoning and Serve

  • Taste and Adjust: Taste the Paksiw na Isda and adjust the seasoning with salt, soy sauce, or fish sauce according to your preference.
  • Serve Hot: Serve Paksiw na Isda hot with steamed rice. Enjoy this tangy and savory Filipino dish!

Tips:

  • Variations: You can add vegetables like eggplant or bitter melon (ampalaya) to add more flavor and texture to the dish.
  • Storing: Paksiw na Isda tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to develop. Store leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 2-3 days.

Paksiw na Isda is a comforting and flavorful dish that’s easy to prepare and perfect for sharing with family and friends. Enjoy your homemade Paksiw na Isda!
