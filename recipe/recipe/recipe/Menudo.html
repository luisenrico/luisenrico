How to Make Pork Menudo: A Step-by-Step Guide

Pork Menudo is a popular Filipino dish known for its rich tomato sauce and hearty ingredients. It's typically served with steamed rice and is perfect for gatherings or family meals. Here’s how you can make Pork Menudo at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork shoulder or pork belly, cubed
  • 1/2 cup pork liver, cubed (optional)
  • 1 large potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 cups water or pork broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika (optional, for color)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooking oil

Step 1: Sauté Aromatics

  • Heat Oil: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat cooking oil over medium-high heat.
  • Sauté Onions and Garlic: Add chopped onions and minced garlic. Sauté until onions are translucent and garlic is fragrant.

Step 2: Brown the Pork

  • Add Pork: Add cubed pork (and liver, if using) to the pot. Sear the pork pieces until lightly browned on all sides, stirring occasionally.

Step 3: Add Liquid and Simmer

  • Pour Tomato Sauce and Broth: Add diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce (if using), bay leaf, and paprika (if using). Stir well to combine.
  • Simmer: Pour water or pork broth into the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the pork is tender and fully cooked. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to maintain a stew-like consistency.

Step 4: Add Vegetables

  • Add Potatoes and Carrots: Add diced potatoes and carrots to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  • Cook Until Vegetables Are Tender: Continue to simmer, covered, until the potatoes and carrots are tender, about 15-20 minutes.

Step 5: Add Bell Peppers and Season

  • Add Bell Peppers: Add diced red and green bell peppers to the pot. Stir and cook for another 5 minutes.
  • Season: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning if needed.

Step 6: Serve

  • Serve Hot: Serve Pork Menudo hot with steamed rice. Garnish with chopped green onions or fresh cilantro, if desired.
  • Store Leftovers: Pork Menudo tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to meld. Store leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days or freeze for longer storage.

Enjoy your homemade Pork Menudo, a comforting and flavorful Filipino stew that’s sure to become a favorite!
