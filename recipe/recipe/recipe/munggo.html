How to Make Pork Munggo: A Step-by-Step Guide

Pork Munggo is a comforting Filipino dish that combines the earthy flavors of mung beans with savory pork and vegetables. Here’s how you can make Pork Munggo at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mung beans (munggo), washed and drained
  • 250 grams pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis), or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 4 cups water or pork broth
  • 1 cup spinach leaves or malunggay leaves (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cooking oil

Step 1: Prepare the Mung Beans

  • Soak Mung Beans: Rinse the mung beans under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the mung beans in water for at least 1 hour or overnight. Drain before cooking.

Step 2: Sauté Aromatics

  • Heat Oil: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat cooking oil over medium heat.
  • Sauté Onions and Garlic: Add chopped onions and minced garlic. Sauté until onions are translucent and garlic is fragrant.

Step 3: Add Pork

  • Add Pork: Add cubed pork to the pot. Sear the pork pieces until lightly browned on all sides, stirring occasionally.

Step 4: Cook Mung Beans

  • Add Tomatoes: Add chopped tomatoes to the pot. Cook until tomatoes are softened.
  • Add Mung Beans: Add drained mung beans to the pot. Stir well to combine with the pork and aromatics.
  • Season: Season with fish sauce and ground black pepper. Stir to distribute the seasonings evenly.

Step 5: Simmer

  • Add Water or Broth: Pour water or pork broth into the pot. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  • Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the mung beans are tender and fully cooked. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to achieve your desired consistency.

Step 6: Add Vegetables (Optional)

  • Add Spinach or Malunggay Leaves: If using spinach or malunggay leaves, add them to the pot during the last 5-10 minutes of cooking. Stir gently until the leaves are wilted.

Step 7: Adjust Seasoning and Serve

  • Taste and Adjust: Taste the Pork Munggo and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, if needed.
  • Serve Hot: Serve Pork Munggo hot with steamed rice. Enjoy this comforting and nutritious Filipino dish!

Pork Munggo is best enjoyed with rice and pairs well with a side of fried fish or grilled meats. It’s a delicious and hearty meal that’s perfect for any occasion!
