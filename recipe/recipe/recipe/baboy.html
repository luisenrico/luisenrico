How to Make Nilagang Baboy: A Step-by-Step Guide

Nilagang Baboy is a hearty and nutritious Filipino soup that’s perfect for cold weather or any time you crave a comforting meal. Here’s how you can make Nilagang Baboy at home:

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams pork belly or pork ribs, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 2-3 pieces potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1 medium-sized sweet corn, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 small cabbage, quartered
  • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy, separated
  • Fish sauce (patis), to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Water, enough to cover all ingredients

Step 1: Prepare the Pork and Vegetables

Prepare Pork: Cut the pork belly or pork ribs into chunks. Rinse under cold water and drain.

Prepare Vegetables: Peel and quarter the onion. Quarter the tomatoes. Peel and quarter the potatoes. Cut the sweet corn into 2-inch pieces. Quarter the cabbage. Separate the leaves of pechay or bok choy.

Step 2: Boil Water and Add Pork

Boil Water: In a large pot, pour enough water to cover the pork and vegetables. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.

Add Pork: Once boiling, add the pork chunks to the pot. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.

Step 3: Simmer and Add Vegetables

Simmer: Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the pork simmer for about 30 minutes, or until tender.

Add Vegetables: Add the quartered onion and tomatoes to the pot. Continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

Step 4: Add Potatoes and Corn

Add Potatoes and Corn: Add the quartered potatoes and pieces of sweet corn to the pot. Simmer for another 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.

Step 5: Season and Add Leafy Vegetables

Season: Season with fish sauce (patis), salt, and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

Add Leafy Vegetables: Add the quartered cabbage and separated pechay or bok choy leaves to the pot. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are just wilted.

Step 6: Serve

Serve Hot: Remove the pot from heat. Serve Nilagang Baboy hot in bowls, with steamed rice on the side if desired.

Tips:

  • Variations: You can add other vegetables like green beans, carrots, or even plantains (saging na saba) for added sweetness.
  • Broth: The broth of Nilagang Baboy is flavorful on its own, but you can enhance it with a squeeze of calamansi juice or a dash of patis before serving.
  • Storage: Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Enjoy your homemade Nilagang Baboy, a comforting and wholesome Filipino soup that’s perfect for family meals and gatherings!
