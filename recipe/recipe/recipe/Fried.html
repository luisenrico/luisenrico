Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a beloved dish that can be prepared in various ways. Boiling the chicken first can help ensure it's cooked through before frying, and it allows for a juicy interior with a crispy exterior. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through making delicious fried chicken using your unique method step by step.

Ingredients:

  • For the Chicken:
    • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (or use chicken parts like thighs, drumsticks, breasts, etc.)
    • Water, enough to cover the chicken pieces
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Optional: Garlic cloves, bay leaves, or other herbs for boiling
  • For the Seasoning:
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, or other preferred spices
  • For the Coating:
    • 1 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1 teaspoon onion powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Vegetable oil, for frying

Steps:

Step 1: Boil the Chicken

Prepare Chicken: Rinse the chicken pieces under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.

Boil Chicken: Place the chicken pieces in a large pot and add enough water to cover them. Season the water with salt, pepper, and any optional herbs or spices you prefer (like garlic cloves or bay leaves). Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and let the chicken simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until cooked through. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (75°C).

Cool and Drain: Once cooked, remove the chicken pieces from the pot and let them cool slightly. Drain them well on a wire rack or paper towels to remove excess moisture.

Step 2: Season the Chicken

Season Liberally: While the chicken is still warm, generously season it with salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, or any other preferred spices. Make sure to coat all sides of the chicken pieces evenly.

Step 3: Prepare the Coating

Mix Flour and Seasonings: In a shallow dish or bowl, combine the all-purpose flour with paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the seasonings are evenly distributed.

Step 4: Coat and Fry the Chicken

Coat Chicken: Dredge each seasoned chicken piece in the seasoned flour mixture, making sure to coat it thoroughly. Shake off any excess flour.

Heat Oil: In a large skillet or frying pan, heat enough vegetable oil over medium-high heat to cover the bottom of the pan.

Fry Chicken: Carefully place the coated chicken pieces in the hot oil, skin side down if using skin-on pieces. Fry in batches if necessary, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry each side for about 5-7 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy. Use tongs to turn the chicken pieces occasionally to ensure even cooking.

Drain and Rest: Once cooked, transfer the fried chicken pieces to a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil. Let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

Step 5: Serve

Enjoy: Serve your homemade fried chicken hot and crispy! It pairs well with mashed potatoes, coleslaw, biscuits, or your favorite sides.
