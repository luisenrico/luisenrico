How to Make Pork Adobo: A Step-by-Step Guide

Pork Adobo is a popular Filipino dish characterized by its tender pork cooked in a flavorful sauce of vinegar, soy sauce, and spices. It's often served with steamed rice and is perfect for a hearty meal. Hereâ€™s how you can make Pork Adobo at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork shoulder, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce (preferably Filipino soy sauce)
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (cane vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the acidity)
  • 1 cup water or pork broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Step 1: Marinate the Pork

  • Combine Ingredients: In a bowl or resealable plastic bag, combine pork cubes, soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, bay leaf, whole peppercorns, and optional sugar. Mix well to ensure the pork is evenly coated. Marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour, or overnight in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.
