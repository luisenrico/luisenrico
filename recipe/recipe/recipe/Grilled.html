How to Make Sinugba Manok (Grilled Whole Chicken): A Step-by-Step Guide

Sinugba Manok is a popular dish in the Philippines, especially during festive occasions and gatherings. It’s known for its juicy, smoky flavors and is typically served with a dipping sauce. Here’s how you can make Sinugba Manok at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (around 1.5 to 2 kg), cleaned and pat dry
  • For the Marinade:
    • 1/2 cup soy sauce
    • 1/4 cup calamansi juice or lemon juice
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • For Basting:
    • 1/4 cup banana ketchup or tomato ketchup
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • For Dipping Sauce (optional):
    • Toyomansi (soy sauce with calamansi juice) or vinegar with chopped onions and garlic

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

  • Mix Marinade Ingredients: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi juice (or lemon juice), minced garlic, ground black pepper, and sugar. Stir well until the sugar is dissolved.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

  • Marinate Chicken: Place the cleaned and pat-dried whole chicken in a large resealable plastic bag or a deep dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring it is evenly coated inside and out. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results.

Step 3: Prepare for Grilling

  • Preheat Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (around 375-400°F or 190-200°C).
  • Prepare Basting Sauce: In a small bowl, mix banana ketchup (or tomato ketchup), soy sauce, and calamansi juice (or lemon juice) to create the basting sauce.

Step 4: Grill the Chicken

  • Grill Chicken: Remove the chicken from the marinade and drain off excess marinade. Reserve some marinade for basting during grilling.
  • Grill: Place the chicken on the preheated grill, breast side down. Grill for about 15-20 minutes per side, basting with the prepared basting sauce and turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the skin is nicely charred and crispy.

Step 5: Rest and Serve

  • Rest: Once cooked, transfer the grilled chicken to a cutting board and let it rest for about 10 minutes before slicing.
  • Serve: Serve Sinugba Manok hot with steamed rice and your favorite dipping sauce (toyomansi or vinegar with onions and garlic).

Tips:

  • Grilling Time: Cooking times may vary depending on your grill and the size of the chicken. Use a meat thermometer to ensure the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F (75°C) for safety.
  • Charcoal Grill: For an authentic smoky flavor, use a charcoal grill. Adjust the heat by moving the coals to control the temperature.

Enjoy your homemade Sinugba Manok, a flavorful and festive dish that’s perfect for sharing with family and friends!
