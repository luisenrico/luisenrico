How to Make Lechon Paksiw: A Step-by-Step Guide

Lechon Paksiw is a delicious way to enjoy leftover roast pork, transforming it into a flavorful and saucy dish. Here’s how you can make Lechon Paksiw at home:

Ingredients:

  • Leftover lechon or roast pork, chopped into serving pieces
  • 1 cup vinegar (cane vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 head garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorns
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cooking oil

Step 1: Prepare the Sauce

  • Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a small amount of cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic and chopped onion until softened and aromatic.
  • Add Vinegar: Pour vinegar into the pot. Bring to a boil and let it simmer for a few minutes to reduce the acidity.
  • Add Sugar and Soy Sauce: Stir in brown sugar and soy sauce until dissolved. Let the mixture simmer for another 3-5 minutes to combine flavors.

Step 2: Add Pork

  • Add Leftover Pork: Add chopped leftover lechon or roast pork to the pot. Stir to coat the pork with the sauce.

Step 3: Simmer

  • Add Water: Pour water into the pot. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorns. Stir well to combine.
  • Simmer: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer gently for about 30-45 minutes, or until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld together. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

Step 4: Adjust Seasoning

  • Taste and Adjust: Taste the Lechon Paksiw and adjust the sweetness or saltiness according to your preference. Add salt if needed.

Step 5: Serve

  • Serve Hot: Serve Lechon Paksiw hot with steamed rice. Enjoy this flavorful Filipino dish with a side of pickled vegetables (atchara) or steamed vegetables.

Optional: Cooking Tip

  • Enhance Flavor: Some variations include adding pineapple chunks or banana blossoms during the simmering process for added sweetness and texture.

Lechon Paksiw is a delicious way to repurpose leftover lechon or roast pork, turning it into a rich and comforting dish. It’s perfect for family meals and gatherings, showcasing the bold flavors of Filipino cuisine.
